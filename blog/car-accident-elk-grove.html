What to Do After a Car Accident in Elk Grove

← Back to Legal Blog

Being involved in a car accident is a traumatic experience. In the shock and confusion that follows a collision, it's easy to make mistakes that can hurt your legal claim. Here are the critical steps to take immediately after an accident in Elk Grove to protect your rights and maximize your recovery.

1. Stay at the Scene and Call 911

Never leave the scene of an accident. Call 911 immediately — even if the accident seems minor. A police report creates an official record that is invaluable in any insurance claim or lawsuit.

2. Seek Medical Attention Immediately

Even if you feel fine, see a doctor as soon as possible. Many serious injuries — including whiplash, concussions, and internal injuries — do not present symptoms immediately. A medical record linking your injuries to the accident is critical evidence.

3. Document Everything

Photograph the scene, all vehicles involved, your injuries, road conditions, and any relevant signage. Get the names, contact information, and insurance details of all parties. Collect contact information from witnesses.

4. Do Not Admit Fault

Do not apologize or admit fault at the scene — even if you think you may have contributed to the accident. Fault determination is a legal and insurance matter, not a roadside conversation.
