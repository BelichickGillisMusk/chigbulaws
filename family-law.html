Divorce & Legal Separation

California is a no-fault divorce state — you don't need to prove wrongdoing to obtain a divorce. However, the process of dividing assets, determining custody, and establishing support can be complex and contentious. Clifford will protect your rights and work toward the best possible outcome for you and your children.

Child Custody & Visitation

California courts make custody decisions based on the "best interests of the child." Clifford has extensive experience negotiating and litigating both legal custody (decision-making) and physical custody (where the child lives) arrangements. He understands that these decisions will shape your children's lives for years to come.

Child Support

California uses a statewide guideline formula for child support calculations. Clifford will ensure the formula is applied correctly and fight for modifications when circumstances change — job loss, income changes, or changes in custody time.

Domestic Violence Protections

If you or your children are in danger, California provides emergency protective orders, domestic violence restraining orders, and priority consideration in custody matters. Clifford Chigbu will move quickly to protect you. If you are in immediate danger, call 911 first.

Frequently Asked Questions

California requires a minimum 6-month waiting period from the date the respondent is served before a divorce can be finalized. Simple uncontested divorces may be finalized shortly after that period. Complex cases involving significant assets, business interests, or contested custody can take 1–3 years.
Sole physical custody is possible but courts generally prefer arrangements that allow both parents to maintain meaningful relationships with their children. Clifford will evaluate your specific circumstances and build the strongest possible case for the custody arrangement that serves your children's best interests.
California is a community property state — most assets and debts acquired during the marriage are divided equally. Separate property (owned before marriage, or received as gifts/inheritance) is generally not divided. Clifford will help you identify and protect your separate property.
