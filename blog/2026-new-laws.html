2026: New State and Federal Laws You Need to Know

← Back to Legal Blog

As we enter 2026, California and the federal government have enacted significant new laws that may affect your family, business, and immigration status. At Chigbu Law, we believe an informed client is an empowered client. Here's what you need to know.

California Family Law Updates

California has expanded protections for domestic violence survivors, making it easier to obtain restraining orders and modify custody arrangements when safety is at risk. New legislation also strengthens enforcement of child support orders, with enhanced penalties for non-compliance.

Immigration Law Changes

Federal immigration enforcement priorities have shifted significantly in 2026. If you or a family member has concerns about immigration status, it is critical to consult with an experienced immigration attorney immediately. Clifford Chigbu has helped hundreds of clients navigate these complex waters.

Business Law Updates

California's minimum wage increased again in 2026, and new employment classification rules affect how businesses categorize workers. Small business owners in Sacramento County should review their employment agreements and contractor relationships to ensure compliance.

Bankruptcy Law

Federal bankruptcy exemption amounts have been updated, potentially allowing California residents to protect more assets when filing for Chapter 7 or Chapter 13 bankruptcy. If you're struggling with debt, now may be a favorable time to explore your options.
