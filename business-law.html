Every business — from a solo consultant to a multi-location operation — faces legal risks. Business Formation

Choosing the right business structure is one of the most important decisions you'll make. An LLC provides liability protection with flexibility; an S-Corp may offer tax advantages; a C-Corp is ideal for businesses seeking outside investment. Clifford handles all formation filings with the California Secretary of State and helps you understand the ongoing compliance requirements.

Contracts That Actually Protect You

Poorly drafted contracts are one of the leading causes of business disputes. Clifford drafts, reviews, and negotiates client agreements, vendor contracts, employment agreements, non-disclosure agreements, partnership agreements, and commercial leases — ensuring your interests are protected in every transaction.

Employment Law Compliance — 2026

California has some of the most employee-protective labor laws in the nation. In 2026, new minimum wage requirements, updated independent contractor classification rules, and expanded leave requirements affect virtually every Sacramento County employer. Non-compliance can result in significant penalties and class action liability.

  • 2026 CA minimum wage: $17.50/hr statewide (higher in some cities)
  • Updated AB 5 exemptions — review your contractor relationships
  • Expanded paid sick leave requirements
  • New pay transparency and pay scale disclosure requirements

Frequently Asked Questions

For most small businesses in California, an LLC offers the best combination of liability protection, tax flexibility, and minimal administrative burden. Corporations (S-Corp or C-Corp) may be preferable if you plan to seek outside investment, issue stock options to employees, or have specific tax planning goals. Clifford will help you choose the right structure for your specific situation.
At minimum, most businesses need: client/service agreements, vendor contracts, an employee handbook, employment agreements for key employees, NDAs for anyone with access to confidential information, and an operating agreement (for LLCs) or bylaws (for corporations). Clifford will assess your business and recommend the specific documents you need.
Generic online templates are often inadequate for California businesses, which face unique legal requirements. They may be missing critical provisions, use incorrect legal standards, or fail to address your specific industry's requirements. Clifford can review any existing contracts and identify gaps or risks.
