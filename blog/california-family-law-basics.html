California Family Law Basics Every Resident Should Know

← Back to Legal Blog

Family legal matters are deeply personal and emotionally charged. Whether you're considering divorce, navigating child custody, or planning for adoption, understanding California family law is the first step toward protecting yourself and your loved ones.

Divorce and Legal Separation

California allows both divorce (dissolution of marriage) and legal separation. Legal separation does not end the marriage but allows couples to live separately with court-ordered arrangements for property, support, and custody. Divorce permanently ends the marriage. Both processes require addressing property division, spousal support, and — if children are involved — custody and child support.

Child Custody and Visitation

California courts make custody decisions based on the "best interests of the child." Factors considered include each parent's ability to provide stability, the child's relationship with each parent, any history of domestic violence, and the child's own preferences (depending on age and maturity). Child Support

California uses a statewide guideline formula to calculate child support, taking into account each parent's income, the amount of time each parent spends with the child, and other factors. Deviations from guideline support are possible but require court approval.

Domestic Violence Protections

California provides strong legal protections for victims of domestic violence, including emergency protective orders, domestic violence restraining orders, and priority consideration in custody matters. If you or your children are in danger, call 911 immediately — then call our office.
